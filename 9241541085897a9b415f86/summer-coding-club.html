Summer is time for discovery, fun, and games. Our summer coding club combines all three into one awesome experience.  

​Here is what we did during the Summer of 2016

Our curriculum is intertwined with our values. We encourage our club members to break away from the norm of traditional coding lessons by making our lesson plans more interactive. Throughout the summer, club members worked in an environment infused with communication, collaboration, and creativity.  ​

​theIntroduction

We kicked off the 16-day summer club meetings with an introduction to computer science.

Then we taught them some basic concepts such as algorithms, coding, and debugging, followed by functions and parameters. Now these might sound a bit complicated for ages 8-12 but we taught them these concepts through a fun game that doesn’t require any electronic devices called Code-Bots and Programmers (a little something we invented).  ​

​theNextStage

Now that the kids understood what coding is and where it can be applied, they were ready to learn about syntax. Through Hour of Code Lessons with a Star Wars theme, club members learned about events and other aspects of coding. The language they wrote code in is Blockly. This method consists of drag-and-drop blocks of code that actually have underlying JavaScript code. Then, they transitioned into typing code.  ​

​theFirstProject

Club members used Scratch, a programming tool built by MIT for kids, to develop their first project.  They were introduced to the friendly user-interface of Scratch and then they brainstormed ideas for their challenge.  

theChallenge: To create a game or animation with a theme. Club members are encouraged to think out of the box and brainstorm ideas to make their creation come to life and fulfill their mission. This isn't Mission Impossible, it's Mission Innovation.  ​

​Robot Day

After learning basic coding skills through the Hour of Code and Scratch, club members got to put their skills to the test on Robot Day. Two teams competed for ultimate glory by trying to earn the most points in 2 competitions: Coding and programming a robot to run in autonomous mode and using remote control in a relay race game. In the end, the orange team prevailed! Check out our Robot Day blogpost for the highlights of this thrilling club meeting.  ​

​theSecondProject  

Club members embarked on a new journey: Coding a website. They learned basic HTML and CSS skills and created a static website on the topic of their choice. Through this project, not only did they develop their coding skills, but also their design, creative thinking, and communication skills.  

theChallenge: To create a static website on a topic of their choice  ​
