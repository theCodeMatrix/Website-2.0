Curiosity never actually killed the cat.  
We know you are curious.  
​Here are the answers to your questions.  


Q: What age group is theCodeMatrix for?  
A: theCodeMatrix is for kids ages 8-16. Our workshops are usually tailored towards specific age groups.  

Q: Do club members need to have prior coding experience?  
A: Nope! theCodeMatrix is for kids who are either interested in coding or may not have heard anything about it before. The club meetings and workshops are structured to give kids an exposure to what computer science is, where it can be applied, and how to write basic code. Our workshops go into a bit more detail about some aspects of the various applications for coding.  

Q: How much does the program cost?  
A: Nothing! It's absolutely free! We are a nonprofit club and all proceedings go to the club activities and any remaining amount will be saved for future programs.  

Q: How do I sign up?  
A: You can sign up for our monthly newsletter, theCryptogram, on our homepage or under Media. Also, you can follow our Facebook page or subscribe to our Youtube channel. We release information about our upcoming programs on those platforms.  

Q: Are you looking for volunteers to run the program?  
A: We are looking for teens entering grades 9-12 to run new branches and monthly workshops. We are also looking for middle school students (grades 7-8) to become "interns." Interns eventually become club officers when they graduate middle school.  
If you are interested, apply using the form on the homepage or about us page and don't hesitate to email thecodematrix@gmail.com for more information.  
If you are part of another student run organization and would like to partner with theCodeMatrix, then email us!  

Q: Is your club tailored to a specific gender?  
A: Nope! We believe that kids of all genders, races, and socioeconomic backgrounds deserve a great education. We are hoping to bridge the gaps in the work place, specifically in STEM and business, through our projects. We also maintain a gender and age balance in our clubs and other programs. 

Q: Are you looking to open new branches?  
A: Yes, we are looking to expand and have more clubs in new locations. If your elementary or middle school is interested in having a workshop on campus during the school year, email us! We are open to suggestions for libraries and schools (open during summer for programs) that we may be able to hold club meetings in. Tell us more about your idea by emailing thecodematrix@gmail.com.  

Q: You are planning to teach HTML/CSS and other complex aspects of coding and computer science overall to elementary school kids. Do you think they are ready to grasp this kind of knowledge?  
A: Absolutely! Our approach to teaching these coding skills combines lessons and projects with fun and games. We strive to simplify the complex concepts and intertwine them with interactive activities that make coding fun for kids. Also, some programs that we use (Hour of Code and Scratch) are specifically designed for introducing kids to coding. Atom, the text editor we will be using for coding a static webpage, is also user-friendly due to color coding and clean user interface. Our Media team works with our Technical team (creators of the lesson plans) to make every club meeting lively, stimulating, and simply awesome.
